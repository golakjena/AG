Atlanta Job Injury Lawyer

On-the-job injuries occur far more frequently than most people realize and are not necessarily from hazardous work environments. A workplace injury can occur in the most benign of conditions. The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) and the U.S. Department of Labor (DOL) conducted a one-year study to determine the frequency of on-the-job injuries and concluded that over 4 million injuries occurred during the study. Another year-long survey conducted by the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) concluded that health care and social assistance industries reported 675,200 cases of injuries and illnesses; the goods-producing industry sector reported 3,857,400 total injury cases; the private industry sector reported 4,085,400 total cases of injuries and illnesses. This was the Survey of Occupational Injuries and Illnesses.

Have you or has someone you know suffered a work-related injury or illness?

The workers’ compensation program was created to offer economic support for medical expenses incurred due to a workplace injury or job-related illness. It also provides death benefits to the surviving dependents of anyone fatally injured on the job. The program protects these injured employees and their families from financial hardship and economic ruin. A lawyer experienced in workers’ compensation can help you receive the settlement you deserve. They will make sure your claim has the best possible chance of being approved for benefits.

Examples of work-related injuries include:

  • Back injuries
  • Neck injuries
  • Spinal cord injuries
  • Paralysis
  • Closed head injuries
  • Traumatic brain injuries
  • Hand and wrist injuries
  • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome
  • Broken/fractured bones
  • Shoulder injuries
  • Amputations
  • Burns
  • Abrasions
  • Lacerations
  • Blindness
