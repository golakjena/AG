2007 Profile of Fatal Work Injuries by State

Across the United States in 2007, thirty states sustained lower numbers of fatal work related injuries than in 2006, according to the Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics. Nineteen other states, however, reported increases in the number of deaths among workers, while one state, Connecticut, remained unchanged.

The state of Texas incurred those most total number of fatal work injuries by state with 527 fatalities in 2007, which a significant increase from the revised total of 489 fatal work related injuries in 2006. The state with the least number of fatal work injuries was Rhode Island, which saw five fatal work related injuries in 2007, which was a fifty (50) percent decrease in the total number of fatal occupational injuries sustained by workers in Rhode Island in 2006.

The top five states reporting the highest number of fatal work injuries by state are Texas, California, Florida, Pennsylvania, and New York, including New York City. The top five states with the lowest total number of fatal work injuries by state according to the Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics in 2007 are Rhode Island, Vermont, Delaware, District of Columbia, and New Hampshire.
